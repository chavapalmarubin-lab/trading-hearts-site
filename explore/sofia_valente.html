European Union Audiovisual Service · P-007781/01-25A · February 7, 1992

Sofia's entire existence was negotiated into being in this room — every clause in this treaty is a small piece of national sovereignty someone agreed to give up for her.

Euro Banknote Design ('Ages and Styles of Europe')

European Central Bank Archive · Kalina banknote design 1996 · Chosen December 3, 1996
Robert Kalina's winning design for euro banknotes, depicting generic architectural styles rather than real, nationally-specific landmarks.
Sofia couldn't show a single real building from a single real country without offending eleven others — so she was designed to look like everywhere and nowhere at once.

Euro Coin Design by Luc Luycx

Royal Belgian Mint · Luycx euro coin design · 1996-1999
The winning common-side design for euro coins, created by Belgian designer Luc Luycx at the Royal Belgian Mint.
One side of every euro coin says something different depending which country made it — Sofia's other side had to say the exact same thing everywhere.

ECB Duisenberg Farewell Speech Records

European Central Bank Archive · Duisenberg farewell speech 2002 · February 28, 2002
The official ECB speech marking the final retirement of the seven founding legacy currencies' legal tender status.
This speech is the funeral for seven currencies at once — the deutsche mark, franc, lira, peseta, escudo, drachma, and guilder all officially died the same week Sofia became the only option left.
