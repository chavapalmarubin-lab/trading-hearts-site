1861 5 Lire Coin (Florence Mint)

Museo della Zecca di Roma · 1861 5 Lire · 1861
One of the first coins struck under the newly unified Kingdom of Italy's monetary system.
Before this coin, 'Italy' was a collection of separate kingdoms with separate money. This coin is the first proof that unification was more than a map — it was a currency too.

Musei di Genova · Cat. 590 · c. 1860
An original red shirt associated with Giuseppe Garibaldi and his 'Redshirts' who fought for Italian unification.
This shirt belongs to the man who made Matteo possible — Garibaldi's unification campaign is the reason there was ever a single lira to unify at all.

Museo del Risorgimento

Museo Nazionale del Risorgimento Italiano · Museo del Risorgimento collection · Founded 1878
A museum in Turin dedicated to the history of Italian unification, the political movement that created Matteo's country.
Everything Matteo represents — one currency, one nation — is documented here in the city where the movement to unify Italy began.

Where this money travelled

Matteo's lira was born from Italian unification in 1861, minted first in Florence and Turin, and lived through wars, hyperinflation, and its final retirement for the euro in 2002.
