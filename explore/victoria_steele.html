Standard & Poor's 500 Index Launch Records

Library of Congress (Business Reference) · LOC Business History Guide reference · March 4, 1957
Historical records of the launch of the S&P 500, the first computer-calculated stock market index, tracking 425 industrials, 25 railroads, and 60 utilities.
Victoria was the first index built by a machine, not a gut feeling — she was designed from day one to never lie about what the market was actually doing.

New York Stock Exchange · NYSE Building, National Historic Landmark · Built 1901-1903

Charging Bull Sculpture

Public art installation · Charging Bull, Arturo Di Modica 1989 · Installed December 15, 1989
Arturo Di Modica's iconic bronze bull sculpture, installed near Wall Street after the 1987 Black Monday crash as a symbol of market resilience.
Installed right after a crash, this bull is basically Victoria's own defiant expression — a reminder that she always finds a way to climb back.

Smithsonian Stock Ticker Artifact

Smithsonian National Museum of American History · nmah_703474 · 19th-20th century
An original mechanical stock ticker machine, the analog ancestor of the real-time data feeds Victoria is built from today.
Before Victoria existed as a number on a screen, this clunky machine was the only way anyone found out how the market was actually doing.

Lord & Taylor Gray Flannel Suit (1950)

Metropolitan Museum of Art · 159455 · 1950
A classic postwar American gray flannel business suit, symbol of the corporate culture that populated the companies in Victoria's index.
