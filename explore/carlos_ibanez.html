First Peseta Coin (Hispania Design)

Heritage Auctions (referenced example) / Museo Casa de la Moneda · 1869 Peseta Hispania · 1869
The first official peseta coin, designed by Luis Marchionni, featuring the allegorical figure of Hispania.
Carlos was born out of revolution — this coin replaced royal portraits with an idea, a symbol of a country trying to imagine itself without a king.

Goya's Saturn Devouring His Son

Museo Nacional del Prado · P000763 · 1819-1823
One of Francisco Goya's darkest 'Black Paintings,' depicting the Roman god Saturn devouring his own child.
Painted decades before Carlos existed, but it captures exactly the mood of the era that created him — a country consuming its own past to make room for something new.
