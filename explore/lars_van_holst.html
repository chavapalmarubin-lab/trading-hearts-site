VOC Share Certificate

Westfries Archief · VOC aandeel 1606 · September 9, 1606
The oldest known share certificate in the world, issued by the Dutch East India Company.
This piece of paper is the actual birth certificate of the modern stock market — the moment ownership of a company first became something you could buy, sell, and trade like a coin.

Regents of the Old Men's Almshouse

Frans Hals Museum · WGA11182 · 1664
A Frans Hals group portrait of the civic regents who administered Dutch charitable and financial institutions.
These are the faces of the men who ran Amsterdam's money — stern, wealthy, and utterly certain the whole world was watching how they managed it.

Bank of Amsterdam Ledger Records

Dutch National Archives · Bank of Amsterdam records · 1609 onward
Surviving administrative ledgers from the Bank of Amsterdam, one of the world's first central banks.
Every entry in these ledgers is a tiny proof that trust, once written down carefully enough, can move more money than gold ever could.
