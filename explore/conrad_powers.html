1793 Chain Cent

Smithsonian National Museum of American History · nmah_1075132 · 1793
One of the very first coins struck by the newly established United States Mint, featuring a 15-link chain design.
The chain was supposed to symbolize unity between states — people hated it because it looked like slavery's chains instead. Even Conrad's very first coin sparked controversy.
