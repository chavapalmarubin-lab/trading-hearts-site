Holey Dollar

Museums Victoria · 55645 · 1813
An early Australian coin created by cutting the center out of a Spanish dollar to prevent hoarding and export.
A century and a half before Scarlett existed, colonial Australia was already improvising its own money out of pure necessity — cutting holes in someone else's currency to make it theirs.

First Polymer Banknote (1988 Bicentennial $10)

Reserve Bank of Australia Museum · 1988 Bicentennial $10 polymer note · 1988
The world's first polymer banknote, designed by Harry Williamson with technology developed by Australia's CSIRO.
Scarlett didn't just adopt currency technology — she invented it. This note is the reason nearly every country's banknotes today resist counterfeiting the way they do.

Royal Australian Mint Opening Records

Royal Australian Mint · RAM founding records 1965 · February 22, 1965
Official records of the opening of the Royal Australian Mint in Canberra, established just before decimal currency's launch.
This mint was built specifically to give Scarlett her own physical body — coins struck by Australians, for Australians, for the first time.
