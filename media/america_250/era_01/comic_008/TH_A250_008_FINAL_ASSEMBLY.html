"Western Pennsylvania, 1794. The farmers have not paid the whiskey tax in two years. They have tarred revenue collectors. They have burned a tax inspector's home to the frame. Conrad has sent letters. He has sent marshals. He has sent threats wrapped in legal language. None of it held. Tonight he has sent the army — thirteen thousand men, more than Washington commanded at Yorktown — to collect seven cents a gallon from the men who bled so he could exist."
