Page 1Comic QA PASS
The lenders arrive — the only lit window in the rain
"Philadelphia. October 1781. Cornwallis had surrendered at Yorktown. But a war won on borrowed money is not yet a war won — it is a bill, not yet read. Tonight the lenders came to read it to him."
Conrad: "Every light on this street went dark hours ago. Mine is still burning. Someone has to be the one who stays. Someone always has to count what it cost."
Isabelle: "Poor boy. He thinks the hard part was the war."
Page 2Comic QA PASS
Isabelle enters — the gift with a blade in it
Isabelle: "France did not save your revolution, Monsieur. France *invested* in your enemy's grief." · Conrad: "And if I cannot repay it?" · Isabelle: "Then you will simply belong to me a little longer. Most arrangements do."
Conrad: "She walked in like she already owns the floor. The terrible thing is I need her to."
Page 3Comic QA PASS
Lars prices the room
Lars: "Amsterdam will lend where Paris lends. We do not believe in your cause, Mister Powers. We believe in your desperation. It is more reliable." · Conrad: "You make it sound like a virtue." · Lars: "It is the only one that pays on time."
Conrad: "He read my worst page in three seconds and did not flinch. I should learn to do that. I think tonight I will start."
Lars: "He is ashamed of the numbers. Good. Shame is the cheapest collateral there is."
Page 4Comic QA PASS
Aurora, who was always in the room
"The franc and the guilder were paper agreements to deliver a thing. She was the thing. Every loan in that room was, in the end, a promise about her."
Aurora: "They lend you their names, Conrad. I am what their names are afraid of. Borrow all you like. In the end, every ledger settles in me."
Conrad: "She was in the room the whole time and I mistook her for the furniture. That is the most expensive mistake a man can make. I will not make it twice."
Page 5Comic QA PASS
The terms — Conrad between his creditors
Isabelle: "Sign, and you are a nation." · Lars: "Sign, and you are a debtor. They are the same signature." · Conrad: "...Then I will be the first country built entirely out of what it owes."
Conrad: "Two candles, two creditors, two shadows of me on the wall. Neither of them is the man who walked in here. Sign it anyway."
Page 6Comic QA PASS
Isabelle's devastation, beautifully said
Isabelle: "You think tonight we made you free. We made you *ours*. The most loyal thing in this world, Conrad, is a debtor who still believes the money was a kindness."
Conrad: "She is right. That is the unbearable part. And the part I will never tell her: I do not mind being owned, if owning me is what finally makes me real."
Page 7Comic QA PASS
Aurora's old, old truth
Aurora: "Debt is not the opposite of wealth, child. It is the first wealth a new thing can hold. I have watched empires bury me and dig me up again. You are not the first to mistake a leash for a lifeline. You will not be the last."
Conrad: "Five thousand years in her eyes and not one ounce of pity. I think that is the kindest thing anyone has done for me tonight."
Page 8Comic QA PASS
THE LINE — Conrad, changed, and the room that watched it happen
Conrad: "So this is what a future tastes like. Borrowed. And sweeter for it." · Isabelle: "...We have made something that will outgrow us." · Lars: "Everything we lend to does. We simply collect first."
Conrad: "Remember this hand. Remember that it shook. One day the whole world will owe me, and I will tell them I never feared a thing. This is the lie I am choosing right now, with the ink still wet."
Page 9Comic QA PASS
Aftermath — the lenders into the rain, Conrad and the debt
"He would borrow again. From her, from them, from strangers not yet born — for wars and canals and railways and a thousand futures. He never stopped chasing that first taste. The debt was never the problem. It was always the point. And Isabelle, in the doorway, already knew she had not bought a debtor. She had crowned a rival."
Aurora: "Welcome to the table, Conrad. It settles in me. It always has."
Conrad: "She thinks the table is hers. They think the leash is theirs. Let them. I have just learned the only thing that matters: the one who owes the most, if he never stops owing, ends up owning everyone. Borrow again. Borrow forever. Never repay the whole of it. That is the trick. That is the country."
