Conrad turns — the need to be witnessed
Conrad: “I have just told the most powerful empire on earth that I would rather starve free than eat at her table.”
Aurora: “There. That need. The need for me to witness it. He will call it liberty for the rest of his life and never understand it was something else entirely.”

Aurora's face — recognition, not reassurance
"She did not sit. Standing was how she measured a man — by whether his certainty could hold its posture in her presence."
Aurora: “You have no idea what you’ve started.” · Conrad: “Tell me I’m right.”
Aurora: “I don’t tell men they’re right. I stay, or I leave. You’ll know which.”
Page 5Comic QA PASS
Cut to London — Charlotte at the window
Nameplate: CHARLOTTE WINDSOR · The British Pound
"CUT TO LONDON. Charlotte Windsor has stood at this window for eleven minutes. This is the eleven-minute version of grief."
Charlotte: “He could not even wait for the ink to dry before announcing it. That haste. That absolute, unbearable confidence that the world will rearrange itself around his hunger.”
Page 6Comic QA PASS
Charlotte turns from the window — she says the name
Charlotte: “No navy. No treasury. No Aurora.”
"She says ‘Aurora’ the way certain women say a man’s name after midnight."
Charlotte: “She was there. Of course she was there. She is always there when something young and reckless announces itself on the world stage. I have watched her do this with Florence. With Amsterdam. With every bright, doomed beginning.”
Page 7Comic QA PASS
Split panel — Philadelphia / London
Conrad: “When they write this down, I want them to record that you were in the room.”
Aurora: “He wants to be witnessed. He wants me to make it real. He does not yet understand that wanting me to watch is the first debt — and I have never in five thousand years forgotten a debt.”
Charlotte: “Reckless men become expensive when they survive.”
Page 8Comic QA PASS
Two hands on the empty ledger — the volatility beat
"He showed her his empty treasury the way a man shows a woman the house he has not yet built. Not as apology. As invitation."
Aurora: “I have watched men sign things before. Constitutions. Treaties. Surrenders. The ones who survive are never the ones who were ready.”
Conrad: “Then tell Charlotte I wasn’t ready. Tell her I did it anyway.”
Aurora: “He said her name. Not mine. In the most important room of his life, in the hour he was born — he said hers.”
